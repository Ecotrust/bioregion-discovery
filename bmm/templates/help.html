Logging In

Registration and Logging In

If you have not yet registered for the tool, you will want to do that now. Select the 'Register' button (top-right) and fill out the required form.

Once the form has been submitted, you will receive an email that enables you to activate your account. Click on the link provided in the email and log in to the site.

You are now ready to create your own Bioregion.

Basic Usage

Creating Bioregions

To begin creating your own bioregion, you will want to locate your home on the map.

Fly Here

Enter the population center nearest your home into the text field and click the 'Fly Here' button.

Mouse Controls

The navigation controls in the upper-right corner of the map can be used to move around the map. Additionally, the Google Earth tutorial provides the basics of navigating the map.

<<back to top

Create New

Once you have found your home location on the map click 'Create New' near the top of the left panel and select 'Bioregion' from the drop down list.

This will bring up the 'New Bioregion' form.

To complete this form, you will need to navigate through three panels.

Step 1 Select a Starting Point.

Select a starting point by selecting the 'Click to Set Starting Point' button and clicking your home location on the map. If you wish to change the location of the placemark, simply select the placemark with the left mouse button, holding the mouse button down as you drag the placemark to the desired location, and release.

<<back to top

Step 2 Specify the relative importance of each of the parameters.

You will want to position each of the slider bars to match the relative value you would like to assign to the given parameter.

For instance, if Temperature should be relatively consistent throughout your bioregion you will want to assign a high value to the Temperature parameter (slide the indicator to the right).

If, on the other hand, spoken Language need not be homogenous throughout your bioregion you will want to assign a low value to the Human Language parameter (slide the indicator to the left).

<<back to top

Step 3 Provide a Name for your bioregion, and optionally a description.

Select 'Submit' and wait while the application generates your bioregion.

<<back to top

Viewing Reports

After you are happy with your bioregion results you have the option of running reports on your shape through the Reports panel. Select 'Reports' or double click your bioregion to bring up the Reports panel.

From here you can generate various reports by clicking on either Summary or Vulnerabilities.

Generate Summary reports by selecting Overview, Language, or Natural Resources.

Generate Vulnerability reports by selecting Climate Change, Socio-Economic, or Natural Hazards.

Advanced

Creating Folders

If you are interested in creating multiple bioregions and managing them in different folders, you can create folders through the 'Create New' menu.

Once you have created a folder you may select one or more bioregion and drag them into (or out of) your folders. <<back to top

Sharing your Bioregion

You are also encouraged to share your final bioregions with others at the Convening. To share with other registered users, select your bioregion, click Edit, and then Share.

Select the group you wish to share your bioregion with and submit.

<<back to top

Viewing Shared Bioregions

You can view shapes that others have shared with you through the Data and Tools panel. To view these shared shapes, expand the Bioregions tree (and subsequent subtrees) under the Shared Bioregions section.
