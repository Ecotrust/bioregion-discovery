Logging In

Registration and Logging In

If you have not yet registered for the tool, you will want to do that now. Select the 'Register' button (top-right) and fill out the required form.

Once the form has been submitted, you will receive an email that enables you to activate your account. Click on the link provided in the email and log in to the site.

You are now ready to create your own Bioregion.

Basic Usage

Creating Bioregions

To begin creating your own bioregion, you will want to locate your home on the map. Enter the population center nearest your home into the text field and click the 'Fly Here' button.

You may also want to manuever around the map with mouse or keyboard controls to more precisely view your home location.

Once you have found your home location on the map click 'Create New' near the top of the left panel and select 'Bioregion' from the drop down list.

This will bring up the 'New Bioregion' form.

To complete this form, you will need to enter a Name and select a Starting Point. Select a starting point by selecting the 'Click to Set Starting Point' button and clicking your home location on the map. If you wish to change the location of the placemark, simply select the placemark with the left mouse button, holding the mouse button down as you drag the placemark to the desired location, and release.

After you have placed an indicator on your home location and provided a name for your bioregion, you will want to position the four slider bars representing the weight you would like the application to each of the four variables, Temperature, Spoken Language, Precipitation, and Vegetation. For instance, if you feel that Temperature is very important in identifying your bioregion, then you will want to increase the weight given to that parameter. If you feel that Spoken Language is not very important in identifying your bioregion, then you will want to weight that parameter with a smaller value.

Once you have given your Bioregion a name and location, and have finished weighting the four parameters select 'Submit' and wait while the application generates your bioregion. <<back to top

Viewing Reports

After you are happy with your bioregion results you have the option of running reports on your shape through the attributes panel. Select 'Attributes' or double click your bioregion to bring up the Results panel.

From here you can generate Summary Characteristic Reports and Vulnerabilities Reports.

<<back to top

Viewing Data Layers

You may also be interested in viewing the data layers that are used to generate your bioregion and create these reports. Some of these data layers are available for viewing through the 'Data and Tools' panel.

By selecting the Data and Tools panel, you can expand the World Bioregional Data tree (under the Bioregional Tool Data section), selecting the layer you wish to view.

Advanced

Creating Folders

If you are interested in creating multiple bioregions and managing them in different folders, you can create folders through the 'Create New' menu.

Once you have created a folder you may select one or more bioregion and drag them into (or out of) your folders. <<back to top

Sharing your Bioregion

You are also encouraged to share your final bioregions with others at the Convening. To share with other registered users, select your bioregion, click Edit, and then Share.

Select the group you wish to share your bioregion with and submit.

<<back to top

Viewing Shared Bioregions

You can view shapes that others have shared with you through the Data and Tools panel. To view these shared shapes, expand the Bioregions tree (and subsequent subtrees) under the Shared Bioregions section.
